    My main approach was to make the interactive dashboard, which will helps the viewer to understand and filter data as they want. For example dashboard allows to choose year and KPIs, charts and also map filter map by years.

    III. Data Cleaning:

    First, I started by looking closely at all the columns in the dataset. I wanted to understand what each column meant and why it was important. This was the first step in making sure I really understood the data. After I finished examining the columns, I made a special table called a pivot table. In this table, I collected the population numbers for each year that was in the dataset.

    This pivot table was like the key to my analysis because it helped me organize the data by different years, which I represented as DATE(YEAR). Then, I did something else. I created a parameter that included all the years I found in the pivot table. This parameter was important because it allowed me to connect it to the charts on the dashboard.

    This connection made the charts interactive, so people using them could easily look at data for different years. It made exploring the data over different years simple and accurate.

    VI. Conclusions:

    The population of the United Kingdom has increased by approximately 21 million people from 1980 to 2023. Furthermore, based on predictions, the population is expected to reach nearly 72 million by 2050.
    In contrast, Poland had a population of 35.5 million in 1980, which grew to around 41 million by 2023. However, the projections for 2050 indicate a decline, with the population expected to drop below 35 million.
    Notably, only the United Kingdom and France are anticipated to experience population growth through 2050. Conversely, Germany, Spain, and Poland are expected to undergo more substantial population declines during this period.
    You can see something important in the line chart. The blue line, which stands for the United Kingdom, and the yellow line, which shows France, are both going up.

    In 1980, China held the top position in the world in terms of population, with India following closely in second place. However, as of 2023, we observe a significant shift, with India's population experiencing substantial growth, nearly matching that of China. This shift has propelled India to the number one spot globally in terms of population.
    Looking ahead to 2050, predictions suggest that India's population is expected to increase by approximately 240 million people. This projection underscores the continued demographic changes and challenges the country may face in the coming decades.
